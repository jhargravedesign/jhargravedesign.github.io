Astrodash

Game Design / Personal

“Your travellers move around and between the boards placing down markers to capture stars and constellations. Zap other travellers to put them out of action, or hide away in your constellations to avoid the same fate. Watch out for the sun as it travels across the board wiping out the stars and, if you’re not careful, your travellers.

Once you think you’ve collected enough points, race to the celestial north pole. If you’ve got it right, you win the game, if you haven’t, well… you could win, but it would be a struggle!”

An astronomy themed, homemade board game invented by my grandad. Since rediscovering the original creation, I have been redesigning Astrodash with my own design take, while staying true to the original.

The logo takes influence from classic sci-fi design. Keeping a modified version of the star-shaped A to create a dynamic swoosh, a staple of space-focused designs.

The board itself has been constructed out of three layers, creating a channel for the 'wipeout' triangles (bright yellow) to slide through without disrupting the game pieces. A darker background colour allows the important gameplay elements to stand out.

As an ongoing project, Astrodash will one day feature new, custom game pieces, rule book and a box big enough to contain it all.
