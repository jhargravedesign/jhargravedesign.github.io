Celestial Tales

There are 88 constellations, each with their own tale, a reason to why a few dots in the sky are joined to represent a creature, king or monster.

The idea behind Celestial Tales was to eventually create a series of books that would both visualise the constellations in the form of illustrations and tell the story behind them. The first book contains the constellations of the Perseus and Orion families. I chose these for the first book as they are the most commonly recognised in the UK’s night sky and the ones I’m most familiar with.

Every constellation is depicted accurately, with various other bits of information displayed including the right ascension and declination grid - the sky’s equivalent to latitude and longitude. They act as a scale, the closer the lines are together, the wider the field of view. More detailed information, useful for a backyard astronomer, can be found at the bottom of the page.

The design takes inspiration from old celestial maps and incorporated rules of proportion from books of that time, while adapting it to a more modern version, reducing the opulence to allow the constellation illustration to take focus.

I plan to continue this project, creating more books that will eventually encompass the full amount of constellations. The idea is to create a new book periodically, allowing the style of each edition to represent a period of time in my design style.
